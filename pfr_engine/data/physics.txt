The theory of relativity usually encompasses two interrelated theories by Albert Einstein: special relativity and general relativity. 
Special relativity applies to all physical phenomena in the absence of gravity. General relativity explains the law of gravitation and its relation to other forces of nature.
When calculating an orbit, you must account for the mass of the objects and the curvature of spacetime. fn calculate_orbit(mass1, mass2, distance)
The velocity of light in a vacuum is a constant, c.
